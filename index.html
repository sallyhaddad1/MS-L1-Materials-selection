Properties of Materials (Basic introduction)


The material characteristics are categorizes in three sections:
1. Physical characteristics Such as: (Density ,Thermal Expansion, Thermal Conductivity, etc.)
2. Mechanical characteristics Such as: (Strength , Ductility, Hardness, etc.)
3. Corrosion resistance characteristics Such as: ( Galvanic corrosion resistance, Sulfur corrosion resistance, Collides corrosion resistance, etc.)

Corrosion resistance characteristics

- Corrosion resistance characteristics is the ability of the material to resist specific corrosion mechanism in moderate or excellent or immune behavior.
- The chemical composition of a material usually determined this ability, other mechanical or chemical processes enhancing this ability such as (cladding, galvanizing, painting , etc.)
- The chemical composition is measured by the relative atomic weight percent of the various elements.
- Numerous elements, metallic and nonmetallic, added to create the desired mechanical characteristics and resistance to corrosion.
- Some of these are intentional additions and some are unavoidable and have to be controlled.
- There is engineering major dedicated to study the material deterioration due to any corrosion mechanism called material corrosion engineer.

Metallic Materials (Ferrous Alloys )

Alloying elements :
A number of other elements that modify the chemical composition to obtain a wide selection of desired mechanical and physical properties, most important alloying elements to iron.
1. Iron (Fe)
 Very ductile and soft, it is very ductile and soft.
2. Carbon (C)
 Produces higher ultimate strength and hardness however, this lowers ductility and toughness.
3. Chromium (Cr)
 Chromium increases steel's response to heat treatment. Stainless steels contain chromium in large quantities, 12-25%, frequently in combination with nickel, and possess increased Resistance to oxidation and corrosion.
4. Nickel (Ni)
 increases strength and toughness more impact resistance Certain stainless steels employ nickel up to about 20%.
5. Copper (Cu)
 added in amounts of 0.15- 0.25% to improve resistance to atmospheric corrosion and increase tensile and yield strength

Non-Metallic Materials


Non-Metallic Materials

1. Thermoplastics :
1. PVC (Poly Vinyl Chloride)
 - UPVC (Un-plasticized or Rigid Polyvinyl Chloride)
* Temp rang: 0C to 60C, W/P 40C
* Pressure range: up to 16 bar
* Size range: up to 12”

- CPVC (Chlorinated Polyvinyl Chloride )
* CPVC is a chemical modification of UPVC
* Similar to PVC in many properties, including strength and stiffness at ambient temperature.
* Withstand higher temperature about (20C) above that for UPVC.
* Temp rang: 0C to W/P 80C
* Pressure range: up to 16 bar
* Size range: up to 12”

2. PP (Poly-Propylene)
 * Temp rang: -10C to 90C, W/P 40C
* Pressure range: up to 10 bar
* Size range: up to 20”

3. HDPE (High-density polyethylene)
 * High-density form of polyethylene , which is more rigid and stronger.
* Temp rang: -10C to 90C, W/P 40C
* Pressure range: up to 10 bar
* Size range: up to 20”

4. PVDF (Polyvinyldene fluoride)
 * Not common
* Temp rang: -20C to 140C
* Pressure range: up to 16 bar
* Size range: up to 12”

Non-Metallic Materials

2. Thermoset :
GRE (Glass Reinforced Epoxy )
* No P-T derating (constant pressure Vs temperature)
* Temp rang: 0C to 110C
*Pressure range: up to 25 bar
* Size range: Min 1” to 36”

- Two different joining methods:
a. Adhesive joint
* Recommended for no-pressurized service due to system rigidity
b. Key-lock joint
* Recommended for pressurized service due to system flexibility

Material Selection Factors

1. CORROSION CONTROL TECHNIQUES
Suitable Materials selection
- Cathodic protection (Making the whole metal surface a cathode)
* Sacrificial anode system
* Impressed current system
- Protective Coatings (Create a film barrier between the substrate and the environment)
* Paints
* Wrapping
* Sleeves
- Chemical Treatment (Different chemical injection techniques)

2. Mechanical integrity
- Material strength
* Wall thickness (Resendable wall thickness relevant to pressure)
- Temperature limits



3. Fabricatbilty
Joining techniques such as threading & welding Non-metallic joining considerations Epoxy coating obstacles
4. Availability
Especially for high alloys or non ferrous materials
5. Cost
Especially for exotic materials such as nickel or titanium alloys
